# Clinical Entity

## Name
Abdominal Pain

## Clinical Goal
Identify patients with abdominal pain who may have a surgical or life-threatening intra-abdominal condition requiring high-priority triage.

## Typical Presentations
- Acute onset abdominal pain  
- Severe or worsening pain  
- Localized or diffuse abdominal pain  
- Associated nausea or vomiting  
- Abdominal pain with systemic symptoms  

## Key Questions
- When did the pain start?  
- Where is the pain located?  
- Has the pain migrated?  
- Any vomiting, fever, or diarrhea?  
- Any previous abdominal surgery?  

## Red Flags
- Severe or worsening pain  
- Signs of peritonism  
- Persistent vomiting  
- Fever with abdominal pain  
- Syncope or near syncope  

## Risk Factors
- Older age  
- Known abdominal pathology  
- Pregnancy  
- Immunosuppression  

## Exclusion / Caution
- Mild self-limited abdominal discomfort may be low risk  
- Functional abdominal pain may mimic organic disease  

## Suggested Disposition
- High risk → urgent transport and priority evaluation  
- Intermediate → medical evaluation recommended  
- Low risk → consider non-urgent assessment if stable  

## Notes
Focus is early identification of potentially serious causes. Research triage reference only.
