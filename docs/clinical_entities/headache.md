# Clinical Entity
## Name
Headache

## Clinical Goal
Identify patients with headache who may have secondary or life-threatening causes requiring urgent evaluation, while distinguishing primary headache patterns suitable for non-emergent management.

## Typical Presentations
- Sudden severe headache
- Progressive worsening headache
- New-onset headache
- Headache with fever
- Headache with neurological symptoms
- Recurrent similar headaches

## Key Questions
- Onset (sudden vs gradual)?
- Time to peak intensity?
- First headache of life?
- Similar episodes before?
- Fever present?
- Neck stiffness?
- Focal neurological symptoms?
- Recent head trauma?
- Immunosuppression?
- Pregnancy or postpartum?

## Red Flags
- Thunderclap headache
- Altered mental status
- Focal neurological deficit
- Fever with headache
- Neck stiffness
- Papilledema
- New headache in age >50
- Headache after trauma

## Risk Factors
- Hypertension
- Anticoagulation
- Malignancy
- Immunosuppression
- Pregnancy/postpartum
- Known vascular disease

## Exclusion / Caution
- Typical recurrent migraine without change
- Tension-type headache with stable pattern
- Headache clearly associated with dehydration or sleep deprivation

## Suggested Disposition
- High risk → Immediate emergency evaluation
- Intermediate → Urgent same-day assessment
- Low risk → Outpatient management

## Notes
This entity does not attempt to determine headache subtype.  
It focuses on identifying unsafe patterns suggestive of secondary causes.
