# Clinical Entity
## Name
Hyperglycemia / Hypoglycemia

## Clinical Goal
Identify patients with abnormal blood glucose who may have life-threatening metabolic derangements requiring urgent treatment.

## Typical Presentations
- Confusion or altered mental status  
- Sweating, tremor, palpitations  
- Polyuria, polydipsia  
- Nausea or vomiting  
- Weakness  
- Seizure or syncope

## Key Questions
- Known diabetes  
- Recent insulin or oral hypoglycemic use  
- Last meal  
- Recent illness or infection  
- Medication errors  
- Alcohol intake

## Red Flags
- Blood glucose < 54 mg/dL  
- Blood glucose > 400 mg/dL  
- Altered mental status  
- Seizure  
- Vomiting with dehydration  
- Signs of ketoacidosis

## Risk Factors
- Diabetes mellitus  
- Renal failure  
- Elderly  
- Polypharmacy

## Exclusion / Caution
- Mild asymptomatic hyperglycemia

## Suggested Disposition
- High risk → Emergency department evaluation  
- Intermediate → Urgent medical assessment  
- Low risk → Outpatient follow-up

## Notes
Always check capillary glucose in any patient with altered mental status.
