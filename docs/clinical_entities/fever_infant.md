# Clinical Entity
## Name
Fever (infant / pediatric)

## Clinical Goal
Identify infants and children with fever who may have serious bacterial infection or sepsis and require urgent evaluation.

## Typical Presentations
- Documented fever  
- Reported fever at home  
- Irritability or lethargy  
- Poor feeding  
- Vomiting or diarrhea  
- Rash

## Key Questions
- Age of child (especially <3 months)  
- Highest recorded temperature  
- Duration of fever  
- Immunization status  
- Feeding and urine output  
- Associated respiratory or gastrointestinal symptoms  
- Recent sick contacts

## Red Flags
- Age < 3 months with fever  
- Lethargy or decreased responsiveness  
- Poor perfusion  
- Respiratory distress  
- Petechial or purpuric rash  
- Seizure  
- Signs of dehydration

## Risk Factors
- Prematurity  
- Incomplete immunizations  
- Chronic medical conditions  
- Recent hospitalization

## Exclusion / Caution
- Well-appearing child with clear viral syndrome and no red flags

## Suggested Disposition
- High risk → Emergency department evaluation  
- Intermediate → Urgent pediatric assessment  
- Low risk → Outpatient follow-up

## Notes
Young infants with fever require lower threshold for ED referral and sepsis evaluation.
