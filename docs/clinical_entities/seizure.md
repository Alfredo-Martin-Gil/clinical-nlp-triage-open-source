# Clinical Entity
## Name
Seizure

## Clinical Goal
Identify patients with seizures who may have ongoing neurological instability or secondary causes requiring urgent evaluation, while distinguishing known, stable seizure disorders.

## Typical Presentations
- Witnessed generalized seizure
- Focal seizure activity
- Postictal confusion
- First seizure episode
- Recurrent seizures
- Seizure with associated trauma

## Key Questions
- First seizure ever?
- Duration of event?
- Full recovery to baseline?
- Focal features?
- Recent head trauma?
- Fever or infection?
- Known epilepsy?
- Medication adherence?
- Alcohol or substance use?

## Red Flags
- First seizure
- Status epilepticus
- Persistent altered mental status
- Focal neurological deficit
- Seizure after head trauma
- Fever with seizure
- Hypoxia

## Risk Factors
- Known epilepsy
- Brain tumor or stroke
- CNS infection
- Alcohol withdrawal
- Metabolic disturbances
- Recent medication changes

## Exclusion / Caution
- Known epilepsy with typical breakthrough seizure and full recovery
- Febrile seizure in child with classic presentation

## Suggested Disposition
- High risk → Immediate emergency evaluation
- Intermediate → Urgent same-day assessment
- Low risk → Outpatient neurology follow-up

## Notes
This entity focuses on identifying unsafe seizure presentations rather than classifying seizure type.
