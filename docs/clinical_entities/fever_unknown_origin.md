# Clinical Entity
## Name
Fever

## Clinical Goal
Identify patients with fever who may have serious underlying infection or systemic illness requiring urgent evaluation, while distinguishing low-risk febrile illness.

## Typical Presentations
- Reported fever
- Chills or rigors
- Fever with localized symptoms
- Fever without clear source
- Fever in immunocompromised patient

## Key Questions
- Duration of fever?
- Highest recorded temperature?
- Associated symptoms?
- Recent travel?
- Immunosuppression?
- Indwelling devices?
- Recent surgery or hospitalization?
- Known chronic diseases?

## Red Flags
- Altered mental status
- Hypotension
- Tachypnea
- Rigors
- Fever in immunocompromised patient
- Signs of sepsis

## Risk Factors
- Advanced age
- Immunosuppression
- Diabetes mellitus
- Chronic kidney disease
- Malignancy
- Indwelling catheters

## Exclusion / Caution
- Short-duration viral syndrome with stable vitals
- Known self-limited febrile illness

## Suggested Disposition
- High risk → Immediate emergency evaluation
- Intermediate → Urgent same-day assessment
- Low risk → Outpatient management

## Notes
This entity focuses on systemic risk assessment rather than identifying a specific infectious source.
