# Clinical Entity
## Name
Urinary symptoms / Retention

## Clinical Goal
Identify patients with urinary symptoms or retention who may have obstructive, infectious, or neurological causes requiring urgent intervention.

## Typical Presentations
- Inability to void  
- Suprapubic discomfort or pain  
- Decreased urine output  
- Dysuria or urinary frequency  
- Overflow incontinence

## Key Questions
- Last void time  
- Lower urinary tract symptoms  
- Suprapubic pain or distension  
- Fever or chills  
- Back pain or saddle anesthesia  
- History of prostate disease or urinary retention

## Red Flags
- Acute urinary retention  
- Fever with urinary symptoms  
- Back pain with neurological deficits  
- Saddle anesthesia  
- Gross hematuria  
- Signs of sepsis

## Risk Factors
- Benign prostatic hyperplasia  
- Anticholinergic medications  
- Opioid use  
- Diabetes mellitus  
- Spinal disease

## Exclusion / Caution
- Chronic stable lower urinary tract symptoms without acute change

## Suggested Disposition
- High risk → Emergency department evaluation  
- Intermediate → Urgent medical assessment  
- Low risk → Outpatient follow-up

## Notes
Consider cauda equina syndrome in patients with urinary retention plus back pain or neurological symptoms.
