# Clinical Entity
## Name
Dysuria / Urinary tract infection

## Clinical Goal
Identify patients with urinary symptoms who may have complicated UTI, pyelonephritis, or sepsis and require urgent evaluation.

## Typical Presentations
- Dysuria  
- Urinary frequency or urgency  
- Suprapubic discomfort  
- Flank pain  
- Fever or chills  
- Cloudy or foul-smelling urine

## Key Questions
- Presence of fever  
- Flank or back pain  
- Nausea or vomiting  
- Pregnancy status  
- Male vs female  
- Prior UTIs or resistant organisms  
- Recent urinary instrumentation

## Red Flags
- Fever with flank pain  
- Vomiting with inability to tolerate oral intake  
- Signs of sepsis  
- Pregnancy with UTI symptoms  
- Male with UTI symptoms  
- Gross hematuria

## Risk Factors
- Female sex  
- Diabetes mellitus  
- Pregnancy  
- Urinary tract abnormalities  
- Recent catheterization

## Exclusion / Caution
- Mild isolated dysuria without systemic symptoms

## Suggested Disposition
- High risk → Emergency department evaluation  
- Intermediate → Urgent medical assessment  
- Low risk → Outpatient treatment

## Notes
Consider pyelonephritis when systemic symptoms are present.
